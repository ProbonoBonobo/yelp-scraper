        This small restaurant has the menu and quality of the larger national chains such as Carino's or Carrabba's.  That being said, you get a decent meal, at reasonable prices. So don't expect fine dining or innovative preparations. Yet, one leaves having dined at a "red gravy" old style restaurant with a full stomach.

        Service was very friendly and efficient. Portions were ample. Wine menu was diverse and fairly priced.

        The four of us shared three appetizers:  a green mixed salad, calamari salad,  and stuffed sausage peppers. The mixed salad had a heavy hand of balsamic vinegar and thus was a little bitter and strong, and the stuffed peppers had a spicy bite to it that grows on you.

        Mains were: chicken parmigiano,  penne arrabiata, baked cod,  fusille & mushroom in a cream sauce. All prepared as expected.

        Considering the limited dining options in the outlying area of Oceanside,  this is a local stand-by that fulfills most expectations.

        5/14/2016

        As long-time North County residents, we'd been meaning to check out Rosina's for awhile.

        Based on the great reviews, we had high expectations it would be a hidden neighborhood gem.  After our visit - I wish I had kept it hidden and never found it - very disappointing to say the least - I don't know if I will get over losing those 2 hours of my life.

        One thing I noticed in other reviews is the difference in experience when Rosina isn't present.  We were unfortunate enough to be there when she was not.   Our server started by telling us how wonderfully Rosina explains the specials from the heart, but she was at their other restaurant.  Then - she asked if we wanted to hear the specials and we said we would.  She proceeded to read them right off her notepad in the most boring and disinterested manner EVER.  If Rosina would've said them from the heart, well, our server said them as if she was a robot without one -- she should've told us how lackluster she felt about them before reading them to us.

        While we perused the menu - I strained to hear another server nearby with blonde hair who did seem to be more interested in regaling her guests with the specials in much more detail.  The specials I could hear sounded better with explanation for sure.

        We ordered the saltimbocca del oro and one of the specials - a 2-pasta dish with arrabiata and another one with a light cream sauce & cauliflower.  The saltimbocca was positively dreadful.  Meat was chewy, tough and flavorless - thank goodness for the proscuitto & cheese that gave a tiny bit of flavor.  The side of vegetables were overcooked and mushy - probably what one would expect from Olive Garden.  

        The pasta special was sad.  The arrabiata had about 10 pieces of penne and the cauliflower fusilli had about 12.  Seriously - I counted them.  I thought it was meager, even for "authentic italian" and definitely had no expectation of an overflowing bowl of pasta (After all, this wasn't Olive Garden, even if the vegetables tasted like it).    Being a "special" - I didn't learn the price till I got the bill and it was then when I realized how meager the value ----- the pasta special was $17.95.  For 22 pieces of pasta that's almost $1 a piece of pasta (Actually .82)!!!  I guess there must've been something extraordinary in the sauce to warrant the cost - but, since the server didn't explain what that was - my bad for ordering it.  

        I won't even touch on the dessert except to say it tasted like something I could have picked up at Costco or Trader Joe's.

        As for ambience - the indoor portion of the restaurant is small and cramped.  The restaurant was only 1/2 full, but, if it were packed, I could see it being uncomfortable.  There are several tables right next to the kitchen which is distracting due to all the servers chattering near the curtain not to mention non-delicious smells (kitchen water? bleach?) wafting from the general area.  Service was generally non-attentive - water cup never filled.  Long waits between courses.  

        Overall - a definite miss.

        We came here for date night and I was excited to try a new Italian restaurant. At first glance this place looked great. I left having mixed feelings about this place.

        Food: We were given a bread basket with a olive oil and sun dried tomato mix for dipping. We ordered the stuffed peppers as an appetizer. Which took a while to come out, we were beginning to think it would end up coming out with the main course. The stuffing of meat was good and the sauce had a nice spice. The outside of the pepper was a little tough and difficult to eat. We both ordered pasta, I got the fettuccine with seafood and he got the fettuccine with meat sauce. The food took about an hour to reach our table. My dish came out warm but not piping hot. And my boyfriend's dish came to the table about room temp not even warm. Both the dishes came out at the same time. The portion sizes were good, it fills you up. The flavor of the food was good but it wasn't anything extraordinary.

        Customer service: When we first walked in we were greeted by a server who told us we had to wait to be seated by the owner herself. So we stood and waited for the owner. It took a few minutes but she came to front and asked if we needed help. We told her we were waiting to be seated and then she walked away. She proceeded to check on tables and filled water containers walking back and forth. Finally came back to the front grabbed some menus and proceeded to the outdoor seating area not saying word. We just assumed she wanted us follow and so we did. It was just awkward standing there waiting to be seated while servers walked passed us. I think a hostess would have been nicer at the front to greet customers. I have to say our server was really kind. She checked on us consistently and took care of our needs.

        Atmosphere: We decided not to sit inside, it was a little too dark and very stuffy. The outdoor area was nicer and its next to a fountain in the shopping center. There are several umbrellas outside and a  white tent in the center (picture posted).

        Bottom line would I come back, yes I would come back again on a different day see if things would be better.

      History

      Established in 2008.

      Her remarkable family of restaurateurs shares Rosina's talent and pride for authenticity. When she and her sister, brothers and their spouses opened their family restaurant When In Rome in Leucadia, California almost 30 years ago, the community welcomed their new recipes. From When In Rome came Salvatore's Cucina Italiana downtown San Diego, Cafe Luna in Rancho Bernardo, Salvatore's in Sun Valley Idaho, Rosina's in Sun Valley Idaho, Castello Delle Serre in Serre di Rapolano, Italy, Carmine's Pizzeria in Oceanside and now Rosina's in Oceanside.

      Meet the Business Owner

      Rosina G.
      Business Owner

      Rosina will tell you she loves cooking as much as she loves people. "When you sit down for dinner with your children, you are assured they will do the same when they are adults," says Rosina. This is why it is so important to create Italian meals with pride, because you are making something for the people you love, your family. In this way, Rosina has always prepared her fresh sauces and baked pastas!
